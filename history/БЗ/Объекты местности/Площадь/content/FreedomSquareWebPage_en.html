In 1795 Minsk became a part of Russian Empire and became a governorate town. As in previous periods, the Freedom Square which was called the Cathedral Square, remains to be the an administrative center of the city. the Jesuit College building became the Governor's House . St. Barbara's, Virgin Mary's and Jesus jesuit church became a parish catholic church Refurbished Town Hall was used for rehearsals of the orchestra of the city. In 1851 building were demolished. In the Male Basilian Monastery Building was located the Minsk Male Gymnasium and parish locations. the Building of courtyard seating was rebuilt by architect Kramer in a classical style. And later There were placed retail shops and the Minsk branch of the Azov-Don Bank. In connection with the liquidation of the Uniate Order all Uniate churches became Orthodox. The Church of Holy Spirit was rebuilt in pseudo-Russian style. It became the Peter and Paul's Cathedral. It used to be Cathedral Church of the city.
